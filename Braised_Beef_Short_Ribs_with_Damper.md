# Braised Beef Short Ribs with Damper in the Dome

**Effort:** 🕐 Weekend Project | **Serves:** 4–6 + freezer portions | **Hands-on:** 25 min | **Total:** 4–4.5 hours | **Start by:** 12:15 PM (for 5:30 PM dinner)

## Ingredients — Short Ribs
- 2–3kg beef short ribs (or boneless beef chuck, cut into large chunks)
- 2 brown onions, roughly diced
- 5–6 cloves garlic, crushed
- 3 carrots, cut into chunks
- 2 sticks celery, cut into chunks (optional)
- 4–6 red potatoes (Desiree or Pontiac), halved or quartered into golf-ball-sized chunks
- 200–300g mushrooms, halved or quartered
- 2 tins diced tomatoes
- 3 cups beef bone broth
- 3 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 tsp paprika
- Salt and pepper
- Olive oil

## Ingredients — Damper
- 3 cups self-raising flour (or 3 cups plain flour + 4 tsp baking powder)
- Pinch of salt
- 1 knob of butter (~20–30g), cold, cut into small cubes
- 1–1.5 cups milk (or water)

## Equipment
- Large Dutch oven (cast iron ideal — make sure it fits through the Dome opening)

## Suggested Timeline (for 5:30 PM dinner)

| Time | What |
|------|------|
| 12:15 PM | Light the fire in the Dome |
| 12:45 PM | Start chopping and stovetop browning |
| 1:15 PM | Dutch oven goes into the Dome, door on |
| 1:15–3:45 PM | Free — check every 45 min, top up wood as needed |
| 3:45 PM | Add potatoes and mushrooms to the braise |
| 4:55 PM | Add wood to bring the Dome temp up for damper |
| 5:00 PM | Make the damper |

## Method — Fire
1. Build a small stack of kindling in the centre of the stone with a firelighter at the base.
2. Once fully caught, push the fire to the side onto the wood rack.
3. Let it burn down to embers — aim for 150–170°C stone temp.
4. Use the woodfire control kit fan to regulate airflow. Feed in a small piece of wood every 45 min to maintain temp.

## Method — Short Ribs
1. Season ribs generously with salt, pepper, and paprika.
2. Heat Dutch oven on stovetop with olive oil. Brown ribs hard on all sides in batches. Set aside.
3. Cook onions, garlic, carrots, and celery for a few minutes until softened.
4. Add tomato paste, stir through for a minute.
5. Add diced tomatoes, bone broth, and Worcestershire sauce. Stir and bring to a simmer.
6. Nestle ribs back in — mostly submerged.
7. Lid on. Transfer to the Dome. Door on.
8. Check every 45 min — top up wood as needed.
9. At the 2.5 hour mark, add potatoes and mushrooms. Cut potatoes smaller if adding later.
10. At 3.5 hours total, meat should be falling off the bone. If not, give it another 30 min.

## Method — Damper
1. Add a couple of pieces of wood to bring the Dome up towards 200°C.
2. Rub cold butter into flour and salt until it looks like rough breadcrumbs.
3. Add milk gradually, mix until just combined — don't overwork.
4. Shape into a 20cm round. Score a cross on top.
5. Place on the stone or a floured tray. Door on, vent slightly open.
6. Bake 20–25 min until golden and sounds hollow when tapped.

## Finishing
1. Remove bones — they should slide out cleanly.
2. Shred meat with two forks.
3. To thicken sauce, simmer uncovered on stovetop for 10 min.
4. Taste and adjust seasoning.

## Serving
- Tear damper into chunks at the table for dipping.
- Serve over rice, with mash, or straight from the pot.
- Frozen peas on the side for something green.

## Notes
- **No wine needed** — bone broth + tomatoes + Worcestershire gives plenty of depth, and the smoke does the rest.
- **Boneless swap:** Beef chuck in fist-sized chunks. Same method, same cook time.
- **Dutch oven fit:** Measure the Dome opening before you start. A 24–26cm round Dutch oven is ideal.
- **Kitchen oven backup:** 150–160°C fan-forced, same method and timing. You miss the smoke but it's still excellent.
- **Damper timing:** If doing ribs in the kitchen oven, bake the damper in the Dome early while the fire's going and reheat later (wrapped in foil, 180°C, 10 min).
- **Potato sizing:** If adding potatoes late (under an hour to go), dice to 2–3cm so they cook through in time.
- **Potatoes and freezing:** They go soft after freezing. Hold back from freezer portions or accept mushier texture.
- **For bone-averse eaters:** Pull all meat off bones in the kitchen before serving.
- **Leftovers:** Freeze in portions. Reheat over rice, through pasta, in toasted sandwiches, or in wraps with cheese.

# Salmon Fish Cakes (Hidden Veg)

**Effort:** 🍳 Standard | **Serves:** 4–5 (makes ~15 cakes) | **Hands-on:** 30 min | **Total:** 1.5 hrs | **Start by:** 4:00 PM

## Ingredients

- 2x 415g tins pink salmon, drained and flaked
- 2 medium potatoes (~330g), cubed
- ~150–200g sweet potato, cubed
- 1 zucchini, grated and squeezed dry
- 1 carrot, finely grated
- 1 cup frozen chopped spinach, thawed and drained in a colander
- 2 spring onions, finely sliced
- 1 egg yolk (for the mix)
- ¼–⅓ cup plain breadcrumbs (to firm up the mix)
- 1 tsp lemon zest
- 1 tsp Dijon mustard
- Salt and pepper

### Coating

- ½ cup plain flour
- 3 eggs, beaten
- 2 cups panko breadcrumbs
- Vegetable oil (for pan-frying)

## Method

1. Boil potato and sweet potato together until tender, about 15 min. Drain well and mash. Spread on a plate to cool slightly — excess steam means excess moisture.
2. While the potato boils, grate the zucchini, salt lightly, leave 5 min, then squeeze dry in a tea towel. Thaw spinach and drain well in a colander, pressing out as much liquid as you can. Grate the carrot.
3. Combine mash, salmon, zucchini, carrot, spinach, spring onions, egg yolk, lemon zest, and Dijon in a large bowl. Season with salt and pepper. Mix until just combined — don't overwork.
4. Add ¼ cup plain breadcrumbs to the mix and stir through. If still too wet to hold shape, add another tablespoon or two until the mix firms up.
5. Shape into ~15 patties, about 2cm thick, using roughly ⅓ cup of mix per patty.
6. Set up a crumbing station: flour, beaten eggs, panko. Dust each patty in flour, dip in egg, press into panko on both sides.
7. Preheat oven to 180°C fan-forced. Heat a thin layer of vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
8. Pan-fry patties in batches, 2 minutes per side, until golden. Transfer to a lined baking tray.
9. Bake for 10–12 minutes until heated through and crisp.

## Serving

- Lemon wedges on the side
- Frozen peas or corn
- Rice, mash, or just on their own
- Tartare sauce or plain yoghurt for dipping

## Notes

- **Squeeze the veg.** Wet zucchini will make the patties fall apart. A clean tea towel wrung hard is the easiest method. Chopped spinach can't be squeezed the same way — drain it in a colander and press out what you can. The breadcrumbs in the mix compensate for the extra moisture.
- **Breadcrumbs in the mix** are not optional — they absorb residual moisture (especially from the chopped spinach) and stop the patties crumbling during crumbing. Start with ¼ cup and add more if needed.
- **Sweet potato ratio:** Roughly 1 part sweet potato to 2 parts regular potato. Too much sweet potato and the mix gets soft and sticky.
- **Freezer friendly:** Yes. Freeze crumbed but uncooked on a lined tray until solid, then bag. Cook from frozen — pan-fry 3 min per side, then oven at 180°C for 15 min.
- **Make-ahead option:** Shape and crumb the night before, cover and refrigerate. Pan-fry and bake the next day.
- **Batch size:** 2 tins makes ~15 cakes. Enough for dinner plus extras for freezer or next-day lunch.

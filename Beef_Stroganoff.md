# Beef Stroganoff

**Effort:** 🍳 Standard | **Serves:** 4–5 | **Hands-on:** 20 min | **Total:** 30 min | **Start by:** 5:00 PM

## Ingredients

- 600g scotch fillet (boneless rib eye), flattened and sliced into thin strips
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil, divided
- 40g butter
- 1 large onion, sliced
- 300g mushrooms, sliced
- 2 tbsp plain flour
- 2 cups beef broth (salt reduced)
- 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
- 150ml sour cream
- Salt and pepper

## Serving

- Penne, egg noodles, rice, or mash
- Frozen peas on the side

## Method

1. Flatten steaks to about 1cm thick with your fist or a rolling pin. Slice into thin strips. Season with salt and pepper.
2. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a large skillet over high heat. Scatter half the beef in and leave untouched for 30 seconds until browned. Flip quickly, 30 seconds more. Remove immediately — it will be raw inside, that's fine. Repeat with remaining oil and beef.
3. Turn heat to medium-high. Melt butter, then cook onion for 1 minute. Add mushrooms and cook until golden, scraping up the browned bits from the pan.
4. Add flour, stir for 1 minute.
5. Pour in half the broth while stirring. Once combined, add the rest.
6. Stir in sour cream and mustard. Don't worry if it looks split — it comes together as it heats.
7. Simmer on medium-low for 3–5 minutes until it thickens to a pouring-cream consistency. Season to taste.
8. Return beef and any plate juices. Simmer 1 minute only, then remove from heat immediately — don't keep cooking or the beef will toughen.

## Notes

- **Beef cut matters.** Scotch fillet (boneless rib eye) or sirloin. Don't use chuck or topside — they need slow cooking and will be chewy here.
- **The quick sear is key.** 30 seconds per side, no more. The beef finishes cooking in the sauce at the end.
- **Kid-friendly:** The mustard is very mild once cooked into the sauce. Reduce to 1 tsp if unsure. Slice mushrooms small so they blend in.
- **Hidden veg option:** Grate a zucchini into the sauce with the mushrooms — it disappears completely.
- **Freezer friendly:** Yes — sour cream doesn't split on reheating. Thaw fully, reheat gently so you don't overcook the beef.
- **Leftovers:** Great over rice, through pasta, or in a toasted sandwich.
